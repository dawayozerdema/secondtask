In Bhutan, you can find various types of properties including residential properties like apartments, houses, and townhouses, as well as commercial properties like office spaces and retail units.

Yes, there are certain restrictions on property ownership in Bhutan. Any property transaction involves the Department of Land Records and Survey, and their approval is required for property transfers or sales.

To buy or lease property in Bhutan, you need to follow a specific process. This typically involves finding a property, signing a lease agreement (for foreigners), obtaining necessary approvals, and completing the transaction with the Department of Land Records and Survey.

You can find properties in Bhutan through real estate agencies, online property portals, or by contacting local property developers, agents, or property owners directly.
