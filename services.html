Improvement

The fourth economic characteristic of land is its ability to be modified or improved with the associated impact on the value. Improvements on the land include buildings, fences or other things that then become part of the real estate. Improvements to land also include access roads, nearby schools, construction of recreational facilities and industries and other factors that make real estate more valuable often without modifying the site itself.

Investment Properties

Investment properties are real estate assets acquired with the primary intention of generating rental income, profit through appreciation, or both. Unlike residential properties purchased for personal use, investment properties are typically acquired as a financial investment with the goal of generating a return on investment (ROI). Investors may seek various types of investment properties, including residential rental properties, commercial real estate, or vacation homes.
